The Chola Samraja refers to the historical reign of the Chola dynasty, which was prominent in south India. 

The Chola Empire reached its peak during the medieval period, particularly under the rule of Rajaraja Chola I and his son Rajendra Chola I between the 9th and 12th centuries.

1. **Historical Background:** The Cholas were one of the longest-ruling dynasties in South India, with their origins tracing back to around 300 BCE.

2. **Capital:** The capital of the Chola Empire was Tanjore, which was a major cultural and political center.

3. **Territorial Expansion:** Under Rajaraja Chola I, the empire expanded significantly, incorporating parts of present-day Tamil Nadu, Kerala, and Sri Lanka.

4. **Cultural Contributions:** The Chola dynasty is known for its architectural marvels, particularly the Brihadeeswarar Temple in Thanjavur and the Gangaikonda Cholapuram.

5. **Naval Power:** The Chola dynasty was also recognized for its naval prowess, establishing trade routes and dominance in the Indian Ocean.

The influence of the Chola dynasty is still felt today and is evident in the rich cultural heritage and history of Southern India.
